Data That Drives the Mission

Two lenses on the same crisis — economic hardship and educational inequity — and why closing these gaps is the work that matters.

U.S. Child Poverty Rate
Supplemental Poverty Measure (SPM) · 2009–2022
12.4%
↑ post-CTC expiry
Source: U.S. Census Bureau, Supplemental Poverty Measure (SPM)

Child poverty is not a background statistic — it is the daily reality that shapes whether a student can focus in class, whether a family can afford supplies, whether a child's brain develops under chronic stress. The 2021 dip to 5.2% during the expanded Child Tax Credit showed that policy works. The rebound to 12.4% in 2022 shows how quickly gains reverse. Mission-driven schools operating in high-poverty communities sit at this intersection every single day.

U.S. High School Graduation Rate
By Race/Ethnicity · ACGR · 2011–2022
~9pt
gap persists
Source: NCES, Adjusted Cohort Graduation Rate (ACGR)

Despite overall graduation rates rising to historic highs, a persistent gap separates white students from Black and Hispanic peers. These disparities reflect decades of under-resourced schools, concentrated poverty, and systemic disinvestment. High-performing charter networks and public schools working with underserved students directly target this gap — and analytics that track, explain, and act on disparities at the school and student level is exactly where I want to apply my skills.
